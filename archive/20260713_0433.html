Landscape configuration and soil properties shape ant-mediated seed dispersal in tropical dry forest agricultural landscapes.CORE4
Authors: Jaiane Maria Silva, M. Lion, Rafaela Cândido de França, B. A. Santos, Helder F. P. Araujo...
Venue: The Journal of animal ecology
Published: 2026-07-06
Abs · PDF
Abstract
Ant-mediated seed dispersal is a key ecological service in seasonally dry tropical forests, contributing to plant regeneration and ecosystem resilience. In human-modified landscapes, however, the quality and quantity of these interactions may be altered by local and landscape-scale environmental factors. We investigated how soil properties and landscape aspects influence ant-mediated seed dispersal in rainfed croplands and natural vegetation remnants within the Caatinga, a dry forest biome in northeastern Brazil. Seed removal and seed cleaning were measured using standardized bait experiments, and interacting ant species were classified by dispersal quality based on body size. Habitat, soil and landscape context jointly shaped ant-mediated seed dispersal. Croplands supported more high-quality interactions, while soil pH and sand content influenced interaction quality. At the landscape scale, water availability and fragmentation reduced seed dispersal components, whereas land-use heterogeneity increased total interactions, likely through small-bodied, disturbance-tolerant ants. Distinct ant groups provided different seed dispersal components (cleaning vs removal), shaped by habitat and landscape factors. While plant recruitment and long-term recovery were not directly assessed, our findings indicate that small-scale rainfed agricultural landscapes can support functional components of ant-mediated seed dispersal with potential relevance for dry forest regeneration.

Abstract
Export-oriented vegetable cultivation in hill ecosystems requires nutrient management strategies that enhance crop productivity while maintaining soil health and minimizing environmental degradation. This study evaluated the effectiveness of integrated nutrient management (INM) practices in a Brussels sprout–lettuce–broccoli cropping sequence under the temperate conditions of the Nilgiris, Western Ghats, India. A multi-year field experiment was conducted at the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR)–Indian Institute of Soil and Water Conservation, Research Centre, Udhagamandalam. Eleven nutrient management treatments were evaluated over three crop rotations for their effects on crop productivity, soil health, and soil and water conservation. The treatments included varying combinations of inorganic fertilizers, farmyard manure (FYM), bio-fertilizers, lime, and crop residue recycling. Pooled analysis indicated that INM-based treatments, namely T 5 (FYM 15 t ha −1 yr −1 + bio-fertilizers + lime + crop residue recycling), T 7 (75% NPK + FYM 3 t ha −1 yr −1 + other INM components as T 5 ), T9 (50% NPK + FYM 6 t ha −1 yr −1 + other INM components as T 5 ), and T 11 (25% NPK + FYM 9 t ha −1 yr −1 + other INM components as T 5 ), consistently outperformed the farmers’ practice (T 2 ). These treatments improved soil pH by 12.3–14.7%, reduced exchangeable Al by 32.5–38.2% and exchangeable acidity by 35.9–50.3%, and maintained 15.8–19.5% higher organic carbon levels than the farmers’ practice. Yield increases of 17.9–39.8% in Brussels sprout, 3.47–4.30-fold in lettuce, and 20.3–63.3% in broccoli were recorded under pooled conditions. In addition, selected INM treatments reduced runoff by 18.2–23.2% and soil loss by 22.2–39.2%, while achieving higher relative soil and water conservation efficiency (50.35–60.01%). The findings demonstrate that INM-based nutrient management strategies improve soil quality, enhance crop productivity, and strengthen soil and water conservation compared with the prevailing farmers’ practice. Treatments T 5 , T 7 , T 9 , and T 11 are recommended, depending on FYM availability, as sustainable nutrient management options for vegetable cultivation in the fragile hill agro-ecosystems of the Western Ghats.
Preliminary Strain‑Specific and Non‑Additive Effects of Single Versus Mixed DSE Inoculation on Rhizosphere Microbiome and Nutrient Cycling Relative to Plant Biomass in Chinese Yam.CORE4

Venue: Journal of applied microbiology
Published: 2026-07-06
Abs
Abstract
AIMS To mitigate Chinese yam continuous cropping obstacles, this study assessed single and mixed dark septate endophyte (DSE) inoculation effects on yam growth, soil characteristics and rhizosphere microbial communities, offering references for optimizing rhizosphere microenvironment of continuously cropped medicinal plant soils. METHODS AND RESULTS 9 DSE strains were isolated from yam roots for single/mixed seedling pot inoculation; We found that growth-promoting effects differed by strain. Acrocalymma and Setophoma terrestris were the most commonly isolated DSE species, but strains with lower isolation rates Paraphoma ledniceana, Amesia atrobrunnea, and Zopfiella pilifera exhibited the most pronounced positive effects on yam root biomass. Increasing the number of co-inoculated strains did not proportionally enhance yam growth but progressively restructured soil microbial communities. However, mixed DSE inoculation as a whole showed no obvious growth promotion compared with the control. Fungal co-occurrence networks exhibited higher modularity and clustering, while bacterial communities had greater connectivity, especially under P. ledniceana and Fusarium sp. inoculation. Biosynthesis was the most abundant predicted function of rhizosphere microbes, with bacteria predicted to be enriched in detoxification and fungi in degradation-related pathways. Two strains of Chaetomiaceae and Periconia epilithographicola increased soil pH, organic carbon and available phosphorus, while available nitrogen decreased after DSE inoculation. Importantly, P. epilithographicola inoculation enhanced mycorrhizal fungi and mitigated microbial imbalance. CONCLUSIONS Single and mixed DSE inoculations regulate rhizosphere microbial assembly and nutrient cycling via species-specific effects. Screening beneficial DSE consortia and characterizing their influence on rhizosphere networks provides preliminary theoretical insights and and candidate strains that may relieve yam replant obstacles in future applications.
Consistent root microbiomes across contrasting habitats in a wild perennial vine.CORE3
Authors: K. Aleklett, K. Karlsson Green, C. B. Andersen, N. Ramirez, D. Kadish...
Venue: Plant biology
Published: 2026-07-06
Abs · PDF
Abstract
While we are beginning to understand that the plant microbiome is important for plant health, we still lack information about how plant microbiomes are shaped across environments and how they influence plant performance, in particular, in wild study species. Here, we examined the root microbiota of the perennial vine Solanum dulcamara, a wild relative of potato with an unusually wide ecological amplitude. Using amplicon sequencing, we characterized root communities of bacteria, fungi, and arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi in eight populations across four habitat types (beach, forest, rural, urban) and investigated the link with habitat and plant performance. We found significant differences in the composition and diversity of the root microbiota across habitats and populations, but a core set of taxa (61% of all bacterial and 73% of all fungal) made up the majority of the root microbiome. The microbiome composition was connected to soil pH and plant nutrients. Even though the investigated populations differed in herbivory and plant performance, the association between plant performance and microbial composition was weak. In conclusion, our results suggest that wild species can have similar root microbiomes across widely different habitats, and that plant performance is not always directly linked to the plant microbiome.

Abstract
Compacted soil (hardpan) conditions in rice fields restrict root growth, reduce nutrient uptake efficiency, and negatively affect crop productivity. This study aimed to examine the effect of manure application on rice root morphology and the improvement of soil physical properties in compacted rice fields. The research was conducted in Banyu Urip Village, Gerung District, West Lombok Regency, using a Randomized Block Design (RBD) with four manure doses (0, 2.5, 5, and 7.5 tons/ha) and four replications. Observed parameters included bulk density, particle density, porosity, permeability, root volume, and root dry weight. The initial soil condition showed a bulk density of 1.28 g/cm³, particle density of 2.10 g/cm³, porosity of 39%, and permeability of 0.8 cm/h, indicating compacted soil unfavorable for root development. The results showed that manure application significantly improved root growth and soil physical properties. Treatments of 5-7.5 tons/ha produced longer, more branched roots, reduced bulk and particle densities, and increased porosity and permeability, especially in the topsoil (0-6 cm). The 5 tons/ha dose was the most effective treatment for improving soil structure and aggregation without decreasing porosity due to excessive fine organic residues.
Integrated mass-balance-based modeling of cadmium in agricultural soils incorporating erosion processes: Future trends, accumulation rates, and dynamic critical loads.PROXY3
Authors: Yongfang Zhang, L. Huang, Wenli Feng
Venue: Environmental pollution
Published: 2026-07-06
Abs
Abstract
Predictive assessment of heavy metal contamination in agricultural soils is essential for food security and ecosystem protection, yet current models primarily focus on vertical transport while largely neglecting lateral erosion fluxes. To overcome this limitation, a spatially explicit dynamic mass-balance model was developed that integrated vertical processes with lateral transport via water and wind erosion, supported by a comprehensive field-based dataset. The model was used to simulate cadmium (Cd) dynamics and evaluate dynamic critical loads (DCL) over 50 years in an industrial-impacted watershed under four scenarios: baseline (A), reduced input (B), straw removal (C), and integrated measures (D). Under scenario A, atmospheric deposition contributed 88.8% of total Cd input, while rainfall erosion accounted for 34.7% of output, confirming its non-negligible role in lateral Cd redistribution. After 50 years, the mean soil Cd concentration increased by 22.0%, 4.8%, and 6.8% under scenarios A, B, and C, respectively, but decreased by 9.5% under scenario D, resulting in a final concentration 25.8% lower than that of the baseline scenario A. Accumulation rate and pollution acceleration were introduced to provide insight into spatiotemporal Cd trends. Scenario D exhibited the highest soil carrying capacity (mean DCL of 555 g ha-1 yr-1), 3.3 times that of scenario A. Atmospheric deposition flux (Ia) and straw bioconcentration factors (BCF) were the most sensitive parameters, followed by sediment delivery ratio (SDR), rainfall erosion modulus (Ar) and the partition coefficient (Kd). The model was validated against historical data, which confirmed its predictive robustness. This study presented an improved watershed-scale framework for the dynamic assessment and management of heavy metals, which aimed to facilitate integrated strategies that target pollution sources and optimize plant-based removal processes. It is designed to inform policy decisions and guide technological innovation in soil remediation.
Cyclable manganese inventory as a mechanistic descriptor for electrolyte design in rechargeable aqueous Zn-MnO2 batteries.PROXY2
Authors: Bing Li, Wanyi Wang, Chen Wang, Dongxv Guo, Chenglin Sun...
Venue: Chemical communications
Published: 2026-07-06
Abs
Abstract
The cycling stability of rechargeable aqueous Zn-MnO2 batteries is constrained by the irreversible loss of active manganese species during charge-discharge cycling. Here, the "cyclable manganese inventory" is defined as the total amount of manganese species that can repeatedly undergo Mn2+ oxidation/deposition to MnO2 during charging and MnO2 reduction/dissolution back to Mn2+ during discharging. Manganese acetate (Mn(CH3COO)2) is demonstrated as an effective electrolyte modifier that enhances reversible manganese utilization through Mn2+ supply and acetate-assisted MnO2 deposition. Consequently, the Mn(CH3COO)2-containing electrolyte delivers a peak specific discharge capacity of 320.7 mAh g-1, substantially exceeding that of the conventional MnSO4-containing electrolyte. To clarify how electrolyte chemistry regulates the cyclable manganese inventory, the Mn(CH3COO)2 system is compared with an isolated acetate (CH3COO-)-containing system and a citric acid (C6H8O7)-containing system. CH3COO- consumes protons and suppresses MnO2 reduction, thereby decreasing the amount of manganese available for reversible cycling. In contrast, C6H8O7 regulates proton availability and suppresses basic zinc sulfate hydroxide (ZSH)-related side reactions, helping to maintain the cyclable manganese inventory during extended cycling. These results support the cyclable manganese inventory as a mechanistic framework for understanding electrolyte-dependent capacity evolution in rechargeable aqueous Zn-MnO2 batteries.
Localized biogeochemistry and seasonality govern carbonate chemistry in estuarine mangrove ecosystemsPROXY2

Venue: Environmental Research Communications
Published: 2026-07-06

Abstract
Sundarbans, the world’s largest contiguous mangrove ecosystem and representing shallow coastal Bay of Bengal of the Northern Ocean, faces dynamic climate variations, including ocean acidification. To delineate ocean acidification from natural pH variations, it is crucial to perform long-term measurements of multiple carbonate chemistry parameters such as pH, total alkalinity (TA), and dissolved nutrients, among others. In the present study, surface water carbonate chemistry parameters, including TA, pH, and dissolved nutrients (o-phosphate and silicate), were analysed monthly between 2014 and 2022 in three pre-defined stations, namely Stn1, Stn2, and Stn3, part of Sundarbans Biological Observatory Time Series (SBOTS) located in Sagar Island, the largest island of the Indian Sundarbans. The observed deviation from the linear TA-Salinity curve in the studied sites of SBOTS showed the influence of freshwater in modulating TA. Generalized Additive Model (GAM) revealed substantial seasonal variability in the controls on TA. During monsoon, salinity was a dominant driver of carbonate chemistry, consistent with enhanced freshwater discharge. In contrast, during the post-monsoon season, primary productivity as indicated by the relationship with Chla, dissolved silicate, was found to exert a stronger influence on TA variability. Multilinear regression (MLR) analysis of calculated pCO2 further supported these seasonal trends. Overall, the findings highlight the importance of season-specific assessments, highlighting the critical role of freshwater discharge in shaping estuarine carbonate dynamics. These insights are vital for predicting the vulnerability and response of mangrove estuaries under future climate change scenarios.

Abstract
Extracellular polymeric substances (EPS) constitute a significant component of soil organic matter (SOM), but the processes by which EPS are transformed and further retained by soil minerals remain poorly understood. This study investigated the chemical speciation of biogenic elements and mineral-SOM associations after EPS adsorption and turnover in typical clay minerals and three soils. Synchrotron-based X-ray absorption near-edge structure (XANES) analysis revealed that incubation induced approximately twice the changes in C and S speciation compared to EPS surface adsorption alone, suggesting that stimulated microbial activity is a more important pathway for EPS to participate in SOM turnover. Incubation with EPS significantly increased carboxylic carbon by 15.9%, while decreasing aromatics by 30.0% across the three studied soils. In-situ Fourier-transform infrared (μ-FTIR) spectroscopy showed enhanced spatial correlation between Fe oxides and organic functional groups (with an average increase in R2 of 90%) after incubation, whereas phyllosilicate-EPS associations were less stable and underwent disintegration in the Fe-rich, low-pH conditions of Mollisol and Alfisol. The differential preservation patterns for various molecules arise from molecular fractionation, with carboxylic-rich compounds preferentially enriched on iron oxides, while aromatic and aliphatic carbon are enriched on phyllosilicates. Overall, EPS stabilizes SOM by stimulating the microbial carbon pump to assimilate native SOM and facilitating its association with iron oxides. EPS priming effect drives greater changes in SOM than simple surface attachment, highlighting the importance of active microbial processes. Different minerals stabilize different organic compounds, iron oxides strongly bind carboxyl-rich compounds, whereas clay minerals favor aromatics and lipids. EPS addition enhances the link between iron oxides and organic matter in soil, promoting a more stable and protected carbon pool. Abstract
Massive agricultural intensification in Merauke Regency as a national food estate demands an integrated assessment of agroecosystem health to ensure sustainable food production. This study aims to conduct a comparative component analysis of soil, water, and climate characteristics in paddy field agroecosystems across Kurik, Tanah Miring, and Semangga Districts. A quantitative approach with a comparative-multivariate analysis design was employed using composite soil sampling across 9 villages. The Soil Quality Index (SQI) was determined using Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce 13 laboratory parameters into a Minimum Data Set (MDS), while site hydrology and time-series hydro-climatological data (2014–2023) were analyzed using comparative descriptive statistics. The PCA results revealed that four principal components explained 86.682% of the total data variability, identifying Potassium retention capacity (chemical) and silt fraction (physical) as the primary drivers of soil health. The final SQI scores ranged from 0.48 to 0.72 (Moderate to Good categories), with an extreme physical constraint of heavy clay fraction (70%) identified in Semangga Jaya Village, inducing poor soil aeration (waterlogging). Spatially, hydrological constraints in Kurik and Tanah Miring Districts were dominated by flooding risks caused by flat topography and high sedimentation in tertiary irrigation canals. Conversely, Semangga District faced double stress from high saltwater intrusion risks (62%) during the dry season and severe soil cracking due to the interaction of high maximum air temperatures (29.10 °C) with heavy clay textures. Furthermore, high atmospheric humidity (>83%) stimulated the risk of blast disease pathogen (Pyricularia oryzae) outbreaks across all study zones. This study concludes that to prevent land fatigue, site-specific management must be implemented through the modernization of micro-water control structures and the regular application of organic soil amendments.
