A paleopathological study of the mandible of Canis etruscus Forsyth Major, 1877, from the Early Pleistocene Muhkai 2 locality (northeastern Caucasus) and the problem of group behavior in early representatives of the genus CanisCORE3
Authors: N. A. Lyubimov, M. V. Sablin
Venue: Proceedings of the Zoological Institute RAS
Published: 2026-06-22
Abs
Abstract
This study presents a detailed analysis of a large canid mandible, identified as Canis etruscus Forsyth Major, 1877, from the Muhkai 2 locality (northeastern Caucasus, Russia). A reliable chronological framework of 2.1 to 1.77 Mya for the specimen was established through integrated paleomagnetic and faunal biostratigraphy of the site's geological context. The fossil, found fully embedded in a consolidated dolomitic matrix, was examined non-destructively using high-resolution X-ray computed tomography. Digital segmentation and virtual 3D reconstruction revealed a significant, fully healed antemortem trauma, interpreted as a displaced mandibular fracture. This diagnosis is based on two key osteological features: a distinct lateral misalignment of the buccal tooth row beginning distal to p4, and a pronounced arcuate deformity of the mandibular ramus ventral to the m1 alveolus. Analogous fracture patterns in extant Canis lupus Linnaeus, 1758 are typically associated with high-impact injuries sustained during the capture of large ungulate prey. The paleoethology of early representatives of the genus Canis Linnaeus, 1758 remains a subject of debate and is incompletely understood. Paleopathological investigations provide a unique line of evidence to address questions regarding complex behaviors such as cooperative hunting and social cohesion. Consequently, the discovery of this severely injured yet healed individual from Muhkai 2 provides compelling and ancient evidence for conspecific care within the Canis lineage. This finding strongly supports the hypothesis that C. etruscus had already evolved as an obligate social hunter by the Late Pliocene to Early Pleistocene, occupying the ecological niche of a diurnal, cursorial pack predator that specialized in hunting medium-sized herbivores.
An Intelligent Robotic Sorting and Predictive Maintenance System Using Machine LearningECO1
Authors: Prof. Thorat Puja Prof. Thorat Puja, Kalyani Dnyneshwar Asane Kalyani Dnyneshwar Asane
Venue: International Journal of Creative and Open Research in Engineering and Management
Published: 2026-06-22
Abs
Abstract
The advancement of industrial automation necessitates intelligent robotic systems that are efficient in operation and proactive in maintenance. This work presents the development of an AI based robotic predictive maintenance and automated sorting system with integration of robotics, machine learning, IoT and mobile monitoring technologies. The smart sorting robot is designed and fabricated by 3D modeling and printing techniques, which is capable of automatically classifying objects by color and shape by Python and computer vision algorithms.To ensure reliable operation, various parameters including temperature, vibration, motion, motor current, and operating hours are continuously monitored using integrated sensors. The collected data is processed through machine learning models for anomaly detection and fault prediction, facilitating predictive maintenance and reducing unexpected downtime. An IoT-enabled architecture is implemented to transmit real-time data to a cloud platform, while a mobile application developed using MIT App Inventor provides live monitoring, maintenance scheduling, data analytics, and instant alert notifications through SMS and email. The proposed system enhances operational efficiency, improves equipment reliability, and supports Industry 4.0 applications in manufacturing, logistics, and smart warehousing environments. Keywords—Robotic predictive maintenance, Automated sorting system, Artificial intelligence, Machine learning, Internet of Things (IoT), Computer vision, 3D printing, Fault detection, Condition monitoring, Real-time monitoring, Data analytics, Mobile application, Industrial automation, Smart warehousing, Industry 4.0.
